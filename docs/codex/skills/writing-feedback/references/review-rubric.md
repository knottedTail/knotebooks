# Review Rubric

Use this rubric to review the writing consistently.

## English writing criteria

- Grammar: correct articles, tense, subject-verb agreement, plurals, prepositions, and sentence structure
- Naturalness: idiomatic phrasing and word choice suited to academic English
- Clarity: clear referents, direct sentences, and low ambiguity
- Flow: smooth transitions between sentences and paragraphs
- Concision: no unnecessary repetition or inflated wording
- Register: match the tone to the genre, from reflective logs to formal exposition

## Mathematical writing criteria

- Precision: state claims at the right level of certainty
- Definitions: define symbols, notation, and objects before using them
- Consistency: use the same notation and naming throughout
- Logical flow: separate assumptions, observations, claims, and proof steps
- Exposition: guide the reader through why each sentence appears
- Scope: make quantifiers and hypotheses explicit

## Feedback priorities

Prioritize in this order:

1. Meaning-threatening grammar or ambiguity
2. Mathematical ambiguity or notation problems
3. Sentence-level awkwardness
4. Paragraph organization and transitions
5. Elegance and polish
