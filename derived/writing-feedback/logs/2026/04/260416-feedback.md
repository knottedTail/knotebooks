## Style and English Feedback

- The strongest part of this entry is its structure: mathematical observations first, then your reflection on writing, then the note about the day. That organization works well for a research log.
- Several sentences still sound translated. The main pattern is literal verb choices, such as "get be accustomed with idiom" or "checked updated papers." When you are unsure, choosing a simpler verb pattern usually gives more natural English.
- The phrase "it is not confirmed" is understandable, but in research English it often sounds better to write "I have not confirmed this yet" or "this remains unconfirmed." That makes the level of certainty more explicit.
- Your reflective paragraphs are clearer when you write directly in the first person: "I noticed," "I think," or "I should." Phrases like "in my guess" sound indirect and less idiomatic.
- The sentence about fragile insights is valuable, but it currently carries too many ideas at once: mathematical research habits, propositions, fragility, and forgetting. Splitting that thought into two sentences would make it easier to read.

## Mathematical Writing Feedback

- In the first bullet list, be careful with singular and plural forms. A sentence about "Chern--Simons invariants of connections" should either stay fully plural or be rewritten around a single invariant of a single connection.
- The sentence about knot invariants and log-decorated diagrams contains an important conceptual distinction, but the long sentence makes the claim harder to follow. It becomes clearer if you first state what is not invariant, and then state what extra structure is required.
- Your discussion of the partition function versus the Chern--Simons invariant is useful because it records a genuine conceptual correction. That section will read more strongly if you distinguish the functional $\mathrm{CS}(A)$, the path integral defining $Z(M)$, and the observable $\langle W_R(K) \rangle$ in three clearly separated steps.
- When you write "it seems that" in the final mathematical sentence, that is the right tone for a research log. If this is your current understanding rather than something you verified from a source, keep that modest phrasing.
- It would help to say explicitly whether "proportional to the colored Jones polynomials" is your interpretation of Witten's framework or a statement you expect from a specific reference. That makes the status of the claim easier to judge later.
