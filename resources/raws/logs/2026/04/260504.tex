We record observation for braided categories.

Let $B$ be a bialgebra in a braided category $\mathcal{V}$.
What we considered is the existence of its opposite multiplication / comultiplication in Majid's sense.
For instance, the opposite comultiplication is not defined by
\[
c_{B, B} \circ \Delta,
\]
it is defined representation-theoretically.
Let denote by $\alpha_{X \otimes Y}$ the action of $B$ on $F(X) \otimes F(Y) \cong F(X \otimes Y)$ where $F$ is a forgetful functor from the category of $B$-modules to $\mathcal{V}$.
Then, the opposite comultiplicaton is defined by
\begin{align*}
    & c_{F(X), F(Y)} \circ \alpha_{Y \otimes X} \circ (1 \otimes c_{F(Y), F(X)}^{-1}) \\
    =\ & (\alpha_X \otimes \alpha_Y) \circ (1_B \otimes c_{B, F(X)} \otimes 1_{F(Y)}) \circ (\Delta^{\mathrm{op}} \otimes 1_{F(X) \otimes F(Y)}).
\end{align*}

The problem is that the existence of $\Delta^{\mathrm{op}}$.
The first insight was that, it always exists because we thought that we always have a functorial isomorphism (in $V$)
\[
\theta^n_V : \mathrm{Mor}(V, B^{\otimes n}) \longrightarrow \mathrm{Nat}(V \otimes F^n, F^n).
\]
It has a right inverse, which is plugging the tensor product of unit in $B$.
However, it is not easy to prove that the right inverse is also the left inverse.
